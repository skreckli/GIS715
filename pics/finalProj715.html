Integrating Science and Policy Creates
Hope for Nocturnal Migratory Birds

Stacie Reckling

In North America, 80% of migratory birds travel overnight. As birds pass through cities on their way between their nesting and winter grounds, artificial light at night (ALAN) has been shown to disorient the birds leading to energy wasted routing through urban environments or even death due to collisions with illuminated buildings. The Audobon's 'Lights Out' program encourage businesses and residents to turn off non-essential outdoor lighting and shade indoor lighting in order to protect nocturnal migratory birds from the effects of light pollution. Currently local Audubon chapters in 44 cities in 24 states participate in this effort. Using NASA's nighttime lights satellite imagery we can visualize whether ALAN decreases during the months of spring and fall migration.

About the map: Participating 'Lights Out' cities are shown as magenta circles overlaid on monthly nighttime lights satellite imagery (NASA's Black Marble product). Maps bordered in grey show months when birds migrate and maps bordered in yellow show months when birds do not migrate.
